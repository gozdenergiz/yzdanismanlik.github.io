Forecasting Lip Landmark Movements Using Time Series Models

About the Study

This study explores the prediction of lip movements during speech using statistical time series models. A dataset of 3,242 frames was created by extracting the x and y coordinates of 40 lip landmarks (80 series in total) with Google’s MediaPipe Face Mesh technology. The analysis applied ARIMA and SARIMA models with different parameter settings. Results showed that ARIMA achieved the lowest forecasting error, demonstrating that lip movement patterns can be effectively modeled with traditional time series approaches

Dataset

The dataset was generated from a publicly available YouTube video with a duration of about 2 minutes (129.68 seconds) at 25 frames per second, resulting in 3,242 frames. Using Google’s MediaPipe Face Mesh technology, the x and y coordinates of 40 lip landmarks (20 outer lip + 20 inner lip points) were extracted from each frame. In total, this produced 80 time series that capture the dynamic movements of the lips during speech.

Methods

This study applied time series forecasting models to the extracted lip landmark data. The ARIMA model was tested with multiple parameter combinations (p, d, q). The SARIMA model was evaluated with seasonal parameters (P, D, Q, s). Forecasting performance was measured using Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE). By systematically comparing these models, the study identified the most effective configurations for predicting lip movements.

Results

The experiments showed that ARIMA outperformed SARIMA in forecasting lip landmark movements. The lowest MAPE was achieved with the ARIMA model (0.0994). The best SARIMA configuration reached a MAPE of 0.1331. X-coordinates were generally predicted more accurately than Y-coordinates, indicating that horizontal movements are more regular and easier to model.
